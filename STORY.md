Some rounds fall apart faster than they should.
Others hold together just long enough to make you believe.

For a moment, it feels like you’ve figured it out.

Then it shifts.

Nothing here is rushed, but nothing waits for you either.
Each round gives you a few choices — small ones, uncertain ones — and somehow they add up to everything.

You’re not reacting fast. You’re thinking ahead.
Positioning, timing, picking just the right piece at the right moment.

Sometimes it works. Sometimes it almost works.

And that “almost” is what keeps pulling you back.

Because every match tightens over time.
Fewer options. Fewer mistakes you can afford.
Until it’s just you and one last opponent, holding on to whatever edge you can find.

When it clicks, it doesn’t feel loud.
It just feels right.

And then it’s over.

So you start again.
